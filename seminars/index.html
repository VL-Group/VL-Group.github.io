Speaker:  Junchen Zhu
Topic:  Towards A Unified World
Abstract:  With the growth of deep learning, many task-specific frameworks focusing on modality-specific data are proposed. To obtain a robust and powerful pretrained model with abundant knowledge accumulated, modality-various tasks and data should be integrated into the training. Huge gaps between tasks and multimodal data make this challenging. This presentation introduces a novel method to unify modalities and tasks to a simple sequence-to-sequence framework. Extensive experiments demonstrate the significant superiority of this unified model to the present ones in performance, efficiency, and transferability. Experience from recent works is summarized at last.
Date:  Feb 25, 2022
Materials:  PPT & Video

Speaker:  Xiaojia Chen
Topic:  Sparse RCNN: End-to-end Object detection with learnable Proposals
Abstract:  Existing works on object detection heavily rely on dense object candidates, such as k anchor boxes predefined on all grids of image featuremap of size H×W. In our method, however, a fixed sparse set of learned object proposals, total length of N, are provided to object recognition head to perform classification and location. By eliminating HWk (up to hundreds of thousands) hand-designed object candidates to N (e.g.100) learnable proposals, Sparse R-CNN completely avoids all efforts related to object candidates design and many-to-one label assignment. More importantly, final predictions are directly output without NMS post-procedure.
Date:  Feb 25, 2022
